1. What HangTime Does

HangTime helps friends find time to spend together without exposing the private details of anyone's calendar.

You connect your calendar (Google, Microsoft/Outlook, or Apple). HangTime reads only your free/busy intervals — it never reads or stores event titles, descriptions, attendees, or locations. When you compare availability with a friend, the app shows only the windows when everyone is free. Nothing about why you're busy is ever shared.

The app is built around three principles:


Security — your raw calendar data never leaves your control. Only free/busy intervals are processed, and they're stored in a server-only location the app's own client cannot read back.
Autonomy — you decide which calendar to connect, who to connect with, and which times to mark off-limits. You can disconnect or delete everything at any time.
Connection — the whole point is to make it effortless to find shared free time with the people who matter to you.



2. Getting Started


Download HangTime from the App Store (iOS/iPadOS) or Google Play (Android).

Connect a calendar (see Section 3).
Set your blackout times so private or protected hours are excluded before you ever compare (see Section 6).

Compare and pick a window that works for everyone.



Tip: You can use HangTime fully on the free tier. Premium adds a longer comparison window, custom blackouts, activity matching, and removes ads (see Section 9).

4. Comparing Availability With Friends


Add a friend. Send or accept a connection request (by username, email, or QR code, depending on your setup).
Once you're connected, open the friend and tap Compare.
HangTime overlays both people's free/busy data, removes anyone's blackout windows, and shows only the time slots when you're both free.
Pick a slot and make your plan.


You only ever see availability — never the contents of a friend's calendar. The same is true for them: they see only when you're free, never why you're busy.


5. Groups

Groups let you find shared free time across more than two people — for example a friend group, a family, a club, or a team.


Create a group and invite people via QR code or invite link.
Groups support up to 20 members.
When you compare a group, HangTime shows the windows when everyone in the group is free, with the same privacy guarantee that applies one-on-one.
The camera is used only to scan QR codes for joining a group. No images are captured or stored.



6. Blackout Times (Weekday & Weekend)

Blackout times are hours you mark as off-limits so they're always excluded from comparisons — even if your calendar shows you as free. This is how you protect time before anyone ever compares with you.

HangTime lets you set blackout windows separately for weekdays and weekends, so you can, for example, keep weekday evenings free of plans while opening up weekend afternoons.

How to set blackouts


Go to Profile → Blackout Times (or the Availability screen).
Choose Weekdays or Weekends.
Set the time range(s) you want marked unavailable (e.g. weekday 9:00 PM–7:00 AM, weekend before 11:00 AM).
Save. Those ranges are now removed from every comparison.



Free tier: preset blackout options.
Premium: fully custom blackout windows.


Because blackout times are tied to your device's timezone, HangTime converts them correctly when you compare with friends in other timezones.

12. Troubleshooting

The Premium page shows an error / purchases won't load

This is usually a store connection or sign-in issue rather than a problem with your account. Try the following in order:


Check your internet connection. The Premium page needs to reach the App Store / Google Play to load products and prices.
Confirm you're signed in to the store. On iOS, Settings → your name; on Android, the Play Store profile must be signed in.
Tap Restore Purchases (Section 11) if you've previously subscribed.
Fully close and reopen the app. Swipe it away from the app switcher, then relaunch.
Check for an app update in the App Store or Google Play and install the latest version.
Restart your device and try again.
Sandbox/test environment note (for reviewers and testers): in-app purchases run against the store's sandbox during review. If the Premium page shows an error, confirm the device is signed in with a valid sandbox tester account (iOS: Settings → Developer → Sandbox Apple Account) and that the products are in an approved/ready state with the Paid Apps Agreement in effect.


If the Premium page still shows an error after these steps, email us at Peyton.rosenberg@emberrosetechllc.com with your device model, OS version, and a screenshot — we'll help right away.

My calendar isn't connecting


Re-check that you granted calendar permission to HangTime.
Disconnect the calendar in the app, then reconnect and re-authorize.
Confirm your provider account (Google/Microsoft/Apple) is still signed in and active.
On iOS, verify calendar access under Settings → Privacy & Security → Calendars (if applicable to your connection method).


A friend's availability looks wrong


Both people need a connected calendar for a comparison to be accurate.
Check that your blackout times are set the way you intend (Section 6).
Time differences are usually a timezone display issue — confirm your device timezone is correct.


I'm not receiving notifications


Confirm notification permission is enabled for HangTime in your device settings.
Check that Do Not Disturb / Focus modes aren't suppressing them.
Toggle the permission off and on, then reopen the app.


Sign-in fails


Make sure you're choosing the same provider (Google / Apple / Microsoft) you used originally.
Check your network connection.
Update to the latest app version and try again.

You're always in control of your data.

Deleting the app from your device does not automatically cancel a subscription. Cancel the subscription separately in your store account (Section 10).


14. Frequently Asked Questions

Is HangTime free?
Yes. The app is free to download and fully usable on the free tier. Premium is an optional subscription (Section 9).

Can my friends see what's on my calendar?
No. Friends only ever see when you're free, never event titles, descriptions, locations, or attendees. The same protection applies to what you can see of them.

Which calendars are supported?
Google Calendar, Microsoft/Outlook Calendar, and Apple Calendar.

How many people can be in a group?
Up to 20 members per group.

What's the difference between blackout times and a busy calendar event?
A busy event comes from your connected calendar. A blackout window is a recurring time you mark off-limits (set separately for weekdays and weekends) that's always excluded from comparisons, regardless of what your calendar says.

How do I change between the monthly and annual plan?
Manage the subscription in your store account (Section 10). You can switch plans there.

How do I get a refund?
Refunds for subscriptions are handled by Apple or Google, not by HangTime directly. On iOS, request a refund through Apple's "Report a Problem" page in your purchase history. On Android, use the Play Store order history. If you believe you were charged in error, contact us and we'll do our best to help.

Does HangTime sell my data?
No. We never sell, rent, or trade your personal data.

Is HangTime suitable for children?
HangTime is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

How do I delete my account and data?
Profile → Delete Account permanently removes your data from our servers (Section 13).
